Chapter 7 vs Chapter 13 in Seattle

Two main consumer bankruptcy options exist: Chapter 7 (liquidation) and Chapter 13 (repayment plan). Chapter 7 typically discharges unsecured debt within 3-4 months but requires passing the means test. Chapter 13 allows you to keep property while repaying creditors over 3-5 years.

Washington Exemptions

Homestead: Washington homestead exemption protects up to the county median home price (varies by county, $172,900-$729,600).

Vehicle: Washington exempts up to $3,250 in motor vehicle equity.

For a complete exemption schedule, consult the Washington statutes or speak with a licensed attorney in your state.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How long does bankruptcy take? Chapter 7 is typically 3-4 months. Chapter 13 is a 3-5 year plan.
  • Will I lose everything? Most filers keep all their property thanks to state and federal exemptions.
  • Can I file without a lawyer? Yes (pro se), but the rules are strict and mistakes can be costly. The Open Bankruptcy Project provides free educational resources at 1328f.org.
