Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Seattle

Chapter 7 (sometimes called "liquidation" or "fresh start" bankruptcy) is the most common consumer bankruptcy chapter. It eliminates most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) within a few months. To file Chapter 7 in the W.D. Wash., you must pass a means test based on your household income.

Who qualifies?

If your household income is below the Washington median, you qualify automatically. If above, the means test applies a deeper income/expense calculation. You also must complete pre-filing credit counseling within 180 days of filing.

What happens to my property?

Most filers keep all their property. Washington exemptions protect: Homestead -- Washington homestead exemption protects up to the county median home price (varies by county, $172,900-$729,600). Vehicle -- Washington exempts up to $3,250 in motor vehicle equity. Plus federal or state-specific exemptions for clothing, household goods, retirement accounts, and tools of the trade.

Timeline

  • Day 0: File the petition + schedules + means test calculation. Filing fee $338 (waiver/installments available).
  • Day 14-21: Court mails creditor notice. Automatic stay halts collection activity.
  • Day 30-45: 341 meeting of creditors with the trustee.
  • Day 60-120: Discharge order entered. Most cases close within 90-120 days of filing.

What Chapter 7 does NOT discharge

Most child support, alimony, recent tax debts, student loans (absent undue hardship), criminal restitution, and debts incurred by fraud are not dischargeable. See 11 U.S.C. § 523(a) for the full list.

Local context: the W.D. Wash.

The Western District of Washington (W.D. Wash.) processes consumer bankruptcies for Seattle. Court website: https://www.wawb.uscourts.gov. The Chapter 13 dismissal rate in this district is 9.3% based on 513,422 cases in the FJC IDB; this is relevant if you're considering Chapter 13 instead.
