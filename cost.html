What does bankruptcy cost in Seattle?

Total cost depends on chapter, attorney choice, and any required fee waivers or installments. Filing the case yourself (pro se) is the lowest-cost option but carries higher risk of dismissal.

The W.D. Wash. accepts the same federal fee schedule as every other district. Fee waivers (Chapter 7 only) and installment plans are available -- see Form 103A (waiver) or Form 103B (installments).

Pre-filing credit counseling

11 U.S.C. § 109(h) requires every individual filer to complete an approved credit counseling course in the 180 days before filing. Approved providers charge $25-$50; many offer fee waivers based on income. The list of approved providers is at the U.S. Trustee's website.

Pre-discharge debtor education

11 U.S.C. § 727(a)(11) requires a second course before discharge. Same providers, same fee range ($25-$50).

Attorney fees

Attorney fees vary widely. Typical ranges in Seattle-area markets:

  • Chapter 7: $1,000-$2,500 flat fee, paid pre-filing.
  • Chapter 13: $3,000-$5,000 typical, often paid through the plan rather than up-front.
  • Chapter 11 individual: $10,000+ retainer, hourly billing.

Many attorneys offer free initial consultations. Always confirm fees in writing before retaining counsel. The bankruptcy court approves attorney fees in Chapter 13 (and Chapter 11) cases via fee applications under 11 U.S.C. § 330.

Filing without an attorney (pro se)

Pro se filing is legal in Chapter 7 and Chapter 13. Federal corporations and LLCs MUST be represented by an attorney (Rowland v. California Men's Colony, 506 U.S. 194 (1993)). Pro se cases have higher dismissal rates -- the W.D. Wash. dismissal rate of 9.3% includes both represented and pro se filings, and the gap is wider for pro se. See 1328f.org for free pro se resources.

Hidden costs to budget for

  • Certified copies of court documents: $11/document
  • Mailing certificates and notarization fees
  • Replacement vehicle if you surrender a financed car in Chapter 7
  • Higher-interest credit / loan rates for 2-7 years post-discharge
Not legal advice. Fees update periodically. Confirm current amounts at the W.D. Wash. clerk's office or attorney consultation.
