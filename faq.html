Seattle Bankruptcy FAQ

How long does bankruptcy stay on my credit report?

Chapter 7: 10 years from the filing date. Chapter 13: 7 years from filing. Both shorter than the perpetual presence of unpaid collection accounts that the bankruptcy resolves. Most filers see credit score recovery start within 12-18 months post-discharge.

Will I lose my house?

Probably not. Washington homestead exemption protects up to the county median home price (varies by county, $172,900-$729,600). If your home has equity above your homestead exemption AND you can't afford Chapter 13 to catch up, the trustee may sell. But most filers keep their homes -- especially in Chapter 13.

Will I lose my car?

Washington exempts up to $3,250 in motor vehicle equity. If you have an auto loan, you typically keep the car by reaffirming the debt (Chapter 7) or paying it through the plan (Chapter 13). Surrendering the car eliminates the deficiency balance.

Can I file pro se (without an attorney)?

Yes for individual filers. No for corporations or LLCs (Rowland, 506 U.S. 194). Pro se filings have higher dismissal rates -- the W.D. Wash. dismissal rate includes both represented and pro se cases. The Open Bankruptcy Project at 1328f.org publishes free educational resources for pro se filers.

What does the means test do?

It determines Chapter 7 eligibility for above-median-income filers. Below-median: automatic pass. Above-median: deeper income/expense calculation against IRS allowances. Failed means test for Chapter 7 typically routes you to Chapter 13.

What is the 341 meeting?

A meeting of creditors required under 11 U.S.C. § 341. The trustee asks questions under oath about your assets, schedules, and means test. Creditors may attend and ask questions but rarely do in consumer cases. The meeting usually lasts 5-10 minutes per case. Held about 30-45 days after filing.

What debts are NOT discharged?

Most child support, alimony, recent income taxes, student loans (absent undue hardship under Brunner), criminal restitution, and debts incurred by fraud. See 11 U.S.C. § 523(a). The brunner test for student loan undue hardship is documented at 1328f.org/brunner-test.

What happens if my Chapter 13 plan fails?

The case is dismissed without discharge, or you can convert to Chapter 7 (if you qualify). The W.D. Wash. has a 9.3% Chapter 13 dismissal rate; failed plans are common, often from job loss, medical events, or unrealistic plan terms.

How often can I file?

Chapter 7 to Chapter 7: must wait 8 years between discharges (11 U.S.C. § 727(a)(8)). Chapter 7 to Chapter 13: 4 years. Chapter 13 to Chapter 7: 6 years. Chapter 13 to Chapter 13: 2 years. See 11 U.S.C. § 1328(f) for the Chapter 13 discharge bar -- the OBP screener at 1328f.com tests refiling eligibility.

Can I keep credit cards through bankruptcy?

Generally no -- credit card issuers usually close cards on filing notice. After discharge, you can apply for new cards (often secured cards initially) and rebuild credit. Keep one card with no balance during the case if the issuer doesn't close it -- helpful for rebuilding the credit file.
